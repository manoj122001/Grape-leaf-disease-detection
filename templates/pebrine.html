Symptoms :



1.The larvae become soft and flaccid.

2.The growth of infected larvae retarded, becomes inactive and vomit gut juice. The faeces become soft with high moisture content. Sometimes chain type excreta and rectal protrusion also observed.

3.Larval head and thorax become translucent.

4.When infected with Bacillus thuringiensis symptoms of toxicity such as paralysis and sudden death are observed. After death, larvae turn black in color and gives foul smell.

5.Some times, the dead larvae turn red when infected with Serratia sp.

Treatment :



1.Disinfect the rearing house, its surroundings and equipments with recommended disinfectant mentioned above.

2.Pick up diseased larvae and dispose them by burning.

3.Provide good quality leaf grown under good Sunlight and recommended inputs. Do not provide over matured/over stored /dirty leaf to the silkworms

4.Avoid starvation, overcrowding and accumulation of faeces in the rearing bed.

5.Rear silkworms under optimum temperature and humidity.

6.Avoid injury to the larvae

7.Apply recommended bed disinfectant as per schedule and quantity.

8.Feed Amruth as per schedule to control flacherie disease.
